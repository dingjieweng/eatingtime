Design Process

Define

  • How many emails have been send?  
  • What triggers the emails?
  • How does that affect out users?
To clarify the problem, I discussed with our cops member, Annie who knows the customer journey the most. After determining the process, I drew a chart to help people understand how email affects our users. The good new is we all follow the same pattern despite we provide service in 7 countries.
User flow of Email
    Exploring in early stage helped us to expose visual conceive for the content and site architecture. To align every stakeholders' opinion better, we collect emails from different platform to consolidate people's thought for this project. After discussing with the team, we all agree on the following points:
    ‍
    • The most important thing about these emails is that they deliver a clear message.
    • Let's keep it simple and clean. Too many context will decrease users' willingness to read them fully.
    There are 3 parts for the email design:  
    1. VISUAL : allow us to use proper visual elements to gain brand preference.
    2. INFORMATION : convey the message that we wanna tell.
    3. HEADER & FOOTER : informations of corporation, policy and Q&A.
    Our target audience are mostly students, Snapask has been using mascot to communicate with them. I created different scenario of the mascot to match the emails' scenes.
